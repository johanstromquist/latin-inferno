# LATIN INFERNO — adventure concept (draft for johan's approval)

A Dante-themed Latin learning game. The student plays a version of Dante, exiled and lost,
who is met by **Virgil** — the Roman Latin poet, author of the *Aeneid* — and guided down
through the nine circles of Hell and back up to the stars. Virgil teaches Latin (the language
of his own epic) as they descend. Every circle is a level; every level reinforces the real
LATLAT01 curriculum and is tagged to the E/C/A grade criteria.

## Why this fits the course perfectly
- Virgil literally is the curriculum: a real Latin author whose lines are level-appropriate.
- The descent gives a natural difficulty ramp (alphabet/pronunciation -> declensions ->
  conjugations -> cases -> syntax -> real Aeneid translation).
- Dante + Rome covers the "kulturbärare" + "senare tiders konst och litteratur" criteria:
  the game itself is an example of Rome's afterlife in later culture (medieval Florence).
- Aeneid Book 6 IS a descent into the underworld — the source Dante himself drew on. Thematic gold:
  - "facilis descensus Averno" (the descent to the underworld is easy) — Aen. 6.126
  - "Tu ne cede malis, sed contra audentior ito" (yield not to evils, go bolder against them) — Aen. 6.95
  - "forsan et haec olim meminisse iuvabit" (perhaps one day it will please us to remember even this) — Aen. 1.203
  - "Arma virumque cano" (I sing of arms and the man) — Aen. 1.1

> Accuracy note: Dante's *Inferno* is written in Italian, not Latin. We use it as the NARRATIVE
> frame and structure. The LATIN content is real curriculum Latin + genuine Virgil/Aeneid lines
> + authentic sententiae. We never present Italian as Latin, and never fabricate Latin.

## Structure — the descent (each = a level / "canto")

| Stage | Dante location | Latin focus (curriculum) | Boss / payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0. Selva oscura | The dark wood; meeting Virgil | Alphabet + uttal (pronunciation); Virgil's self-intro in Latin | Unlock the descent |
| Gate | "Lasciate ogne speranza" | First sententiae/citat; sentence basics | Pass the gate |
| 1. Limbo | Virtuous pagans (Homer, Ovid, Horace) | Nouns; 1st declension; nom/acc; the Roman poets (lore) | Name the poets |
| 2. Lust | Francesca da Rimini | 2nd declension; gender; basic vocab | Translate a couplet |
| 3. Gluttony | Cerberus | Verbs: present tense, 1st conjugation | Conjugation drill |
| 4. Greed | Plutus | The case system overview; genitive/dative | Case-match puzzle |
| 5. Wrath / Styx | River Styx, Phlegyas | Prepositions + ablative | Ferry crossing |
| 6. Heresy | Burning tombs, Farinata | Adjectives + agreement (concord) | Build correct phrases |
| 7. Violence | Minotaur, centaurs, wood of suicides | Tenses: imperfect & perfect | Translation puzzle |
| 8. Fraud / Malebolge | The ten ditches | Syntax: word order, simple subordinate clauses | Multi-line translation |
| 9. Treachery / Cocytus | Frozen lake, Lucifer | BOSS: translate a real Aeneid passage; cumulative review | Climb down Lucifer |
| Exit | "a riveder le stelle" | Final self-assessment vs E/C/A; "diploma" | Emergence to the stars |

## Challenge types (each tagged E / C / A)
- **Vocabularium** — basordförråd flashcard/recall drills.
- **Grammatica** — declension/conjugation/case form drills.
- **Translatio** — read & translate very simple Latin (curriculum's core skill).
- **Hereditas** — "derivation" puzzles: spot the Latin root surviving in Swedish + English
  (directly serves the latin->moderna språk criteria, which name svenska AND engelska).
- **Sententia** — one real Latin motto/quote per circle to learn (citat och sentenser).
- **Lore (Roma)** — short culture/history cards (Hannibal, emperors, daily life, mythology).

Each challenge offers a tiered prompt: E = recognize/recall, C = explain/apply, A = analyze
with nuance — mirroring Skolverket's översiktligt -> utförligt -> nyanserat ladder. The student
sees which grade band their answers are tracking toward (a "betygskompass").

## Form factor (recommendation)
**Single self-contained web app (HTML/CSS/JS), runs offline in any browser, no install.**
- Progress saved in localStorage; resume anytime.
- Dark, illuminated-manuscript aesthetic (age-appropriate, atmospheric, not childish).
- Optional Latin pronunciation audio via the browser speech API or recorded lines.
- Easy to hand to a 17-year-old: just open the file.

## Language framing (recommendation — needs johan's confirm)
**Bilingual, Swedish-led:** UI, narrative, and instructions in **Swedish**; target language
**Latin**; derivation/comparison panels show **Swedish + English** (because the criteria
themselves require connecting Latin to both). English available as a toggle if the student prefers.
